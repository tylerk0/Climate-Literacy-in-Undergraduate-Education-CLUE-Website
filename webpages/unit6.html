Unit 6: Climate Change Effects

Below are Unit 6's Learning Outcomes:

  1. Students distinguish between extreme events that scientists can confidently attribute to climate change and those that are more difficult to attribute to change.
  2. Students describe changes in heat waves, warm seasons, and nighttime temperatures since 1901.
  3. Students describe the impact of change on individuals and society.
  4. Students describe trends in precipitation, drought, snowfall, ice, storms, hurricanes, sea level, infectious diseases, and carbon sinks.
  5. Students reflect on the effects of climate change on various socioeconomic groups in different parts of the United States.
