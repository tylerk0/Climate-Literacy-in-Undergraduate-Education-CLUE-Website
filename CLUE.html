Climate Literacy in Undergraduate Education

(100) / 200 / 300 / 400 : Intro-Level

1–2 Weeks / 6 Units

Summary

Exposure to extreme temperatures and humidity is a significant public health concern to all populations, especially in urban areas, where the urban heat island exacerbates extreme heat and increases heat exposure and heat-related illnesses and death. Traditional ambient weather measurement is obtained through hourly weather stations, such as those at airports. However, fixed-location weather stations do not capture the variability of heat within the microclimates of an urban area and, thus, do not identify more vulnerable populations that need increased resources and mitigation before and during heat events.

Students complete an in-depth microclimate curriculum, collect their own heat exposure data, analyze their data and compare their exposure to others in the class, work with fellow students to interpret results, and apply both scientific and sociological concepts to their results. By completing this curriculum, students learn about microclimate conditions actually encountered by college students. Data was collected by students who wore small temperature and humidity pendants suspended from necklaces during a 2-day interval. Instructors are encouraged to reach out to project lead, Dr. Alisa Hass (Alisa.Hass@mtsu.edu), to explore the possibility of borrowing iButton necklaces, so that students can collect their own temperature and humidity data.

Strengths of the Module

  • Students pull together info from different subjects to understand what affects temperature and humidity in their surroundings.

  • Data sets are used throughout the module, helping students connect scientific data to daily life and community issues.

  • Students explore how human and natural systems interact to create things like urban heat islands.

Students will be able to work with temperature and humidity data on local to global scales. They should be able to:

  • explain how extremes affect health and safety.

  • describe key factors that control temperature and humidity.

  • identify reasons for variation in microclimates and larger regions.

  • use geoscience tools to connect temperature and humidity to sustainability issues.

A Great Fit for Courses In:

  • Sustainability

  • Atmospheric Science

  • Geology

  • Geography

  • Earth Science

  • Environmental Science
